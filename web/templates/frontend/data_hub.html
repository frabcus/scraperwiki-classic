A data hub is the only way to analyse datasets collaboratively.

Just as a CMS helps you manage content, a data hub helps you manage datasets. And because it’s on the web, you can securely give access to the right people whether they’re in New York or New Delhi.

10 advantages of a data hub

  • Help people find datasets (discovery)
  • Load data from diverse sources and formats (ETL)
  • Store the data and index it for querying
  • Browse the dataset like a spreadsheet
  • Create visualisations, such as graphs and maps
  • Analyse the data, to spot anomalies and find trends
  • Data entry and manual cleaning (crowd sourcing)
  • Measure and ensure data quality (provenance)
  • Control access: employees, suppliers or customers
  • Buy and sell data (market)
