Why we need you

There are mountains of New York data out there, but someone needs to liberate and make sense of it if we’re to hold businesses and governments to account.

Columbia University School of Journalism, together with ScraperWiki and the Knight Foundation, are planning a two day event at the beginning of February 2012, to do just that. We need journalists, students, computer scientists, programmers, statisticians and public officials to come liberate some data.

All you need is enthusiasm and a laptop – we supply the food, drink and wifi. Entry is free but space is limited, so check out the three streams below, and reserve a ticket before they’re gone.

Pick a stream

Stream A: The ScraperWacky Races

Take a data story from concept to execution in 12 hours. You and your teammates will identify stories, create hypotheses, and then gather, clean, analyse and visualise the data you need. The best stories, applications or visualisations at the end of Saturday will win prizes.

This stream’s perfect for journalists with or without programming experience, or anyone who wants to dive deep into the production of a data story.

Stream B: Liberate the Data

We want to open up more data, and you’re the people to do it! This stream is the place for experienced Python, PHP or Ruby coders to pick up some high-level scraping skills and then try them out liberating data.

We’ll have a list of inaccessible datasets on hand, ready for you to explore. And, of course, the best liberators will be getting prizes at the end of Saturday!

Stream C: Learn to Scrape

If you already have some basic Python or Ruby skills, but want to know more about scraping, this is the stream for you.

We’ll be holding two 3-hour tutorial sessions: one in Python on Friday morning, and one in Ruby on Friday afternoon.

By the end of Friday you’ll have all the scraping knowledge you need to join a race team in Stream A or liberate some data in Stream B on Saturday.
